Why Our Forests Matter

Health

Forests and trees help us live better and healthier lives

Three-quarters of the world’s freshwater comes from forested watersheds. Countless medicinal plants have originated from forests. Exposing oneself to green spaces strengthens the immune system, reduces stress, lowers blood pressure, and improves focus.

Protection

Forests mitigate many natural disasters and issues

Tree roots hold soil in place and let water easily seep into the ground, stopping flooding and erosion. They can also redistribute water to keep soils moist during droughts. Of course, trees are also crucial for absorbing various greenhouse gases that cause global warming.

Agriculture

Forests are crucial for crop yields and food security

Soil erosion ruins topsoil, leading to poor crop yield and economic repurcussions. The lack of forest cover also makes croplands more susceptible to landslides and typhoons Thus, threatening the livelihood and food security of millions of Filipinos.

Biodiversity

Our forests are home to thousands of native species

Philippine forests boast some of the richest and most diverse ecosystems in the world, with over half of flora and fauna being native to the country. However, deforestation continues to disrupt and destroy these ecosystems, causing hundreds of species to become endangered.
